# 📊 M&A Accretion / Dilution Model – Tata Motors & Sona BLW

## 🎯 Project Objective

Built a merger and acquisition (M&A) accretion/dilution model to evaluate the financial impact of Tata Motors Passenger Vehicles (TMPV) acquiring Sona BLW Precision Forgings.

The model assesses whether the transaction creates or destroys shareholder value by analyzing financing structure, synergies, purchase price allocation (PPA), and post-acquisition earnings per share (EPS).

## 📈 Key Analysis Performed

### Deal Assumptions

The acquisition is evaluated using:

- 25% Offer Premium
- Mixed Financing Structure
  - Stock Financing
  - Debt Financing
  - Cash Financing

The model measures the impact of financing choices on shareholder returns.

---

### Purchase Price Allocation (PPA)

The model incorporates:

- Fair Value Revaluation of Assets
- Asset Write-Up
- Additional Depreciation & Amortization

This reflects realistic post-acquisition accounting adjustments.

---

### Sources & Uses Analysis

The model constructs a complete Sources & Uses schedule showing:

- Total Acquisition Cost
- Capital Raised
- Financing Mix
- Transaction Expenses

---

### Synergy Impact

Expected operating synergies are incorporated into the combined entity's earnings.

The analysis evaluates how synergies affect:

- Pretax Income
- Net Income
- Earnings Per Share

---

### Pro Forma EPS Analysis

The model calculates:

- Pro Forma Net Income
- Pro Forma Share Count
- Pro Forma EPS

and compares them with the acquirer's standalone EPS to determine whether the deal is:

✅ Accretive

or

❌ Dilutive

to shareholders.

---

## 🔎 Key Insights

- Financing structure has a significant impact on shareholder value creation.
- Debt financing increases interest burden and affects EPS.
- Purchase accounting adjustments reduce reported earnings through additional amortization.
- Synergies can partially offset transaction-related costs.
- Pro Forma EPS provides a clear measure of acquisition attractiveness.
